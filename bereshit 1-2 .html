🧠 Syntax: Passive verbal form + coordinated nouns expressing totality and finality.

Genesis 2:2

Vayekhal Elohim bayom hashevi’i melakhto asher asah, vayishbot bayom hashevi’i mikol melakhto asher asah

And God finished on the seventh day His work which He had done, and He rested on the seventh day from all His work which He had done.

🧠 Syntax: Sequential verbal clauses with temporal emphasis and symmetry; chiastic repetition reinforces Sabbath motif.

Genesis 2:3

וַיְבָרֶךְ אֱלֹהִים אֶת-יוֹם הַשְּׁבִיעִי וַיְקַדֵּשׁ אֹתוֹ כִּי בוֹ שָׁבַת מִכָּל-מְלַאכְתּוֹ אֲשֶׁר בָּרָא אֱלֹהִים לַעֲשׂוֹת

Vayevarekh Elohim et yom hashevi’i vayekadesh oto, ki vo shavat mikol melakhto asher bara Elohim la’asot

And God blessed the seventh day and sanctified it, because in it He rested from all His work which God created to do.

🧠 Syntax: Blessing and sanctification formula with causal clause; final temporal reflection closing the Sabbath triad.

Genesis 2:4

Elleh toledot hashamayim vehaaretz behibbare’am, beyom asot Adonai Elohim eretz v’shamayim

These are the generations of the heavens and the earth when they were created, in the day that the Lord God made earth and heavens.

🧠 Syntax: Superscription formula with relative clause and temporal phrase; literary hinge between accounts.

Genesis 2:5
